GearDrop

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2026

Overview

GearDrop helps shoppers discover outdoor gear markdowns, compare recent price history, save products, and create price alerts. This policy covers the GearDrop iOS app and the website at 001.100app.dev.

Information we store

  • Saved products, saved-time price snapshots, notification preference, and Pro status are stored locally on your device.
  • When you create a price alert, GearDrop stores the email address you enter, target price, product SKU, current price, region, product URL, image URL, and an unsubscribe token so the alert can be managed.
  • GearDrop does not ask for account passwords, payment card numbers, precise location, contacts, photos, or health data.

Information we read

The app reads public product and price-history data from the GearDrop catalog. Product records can include merchant product names, prices, regions, product URLs, and images. The app does not read other users' price-alert subscriptions.

Notifications and purchases

This version uses local notifications for alert testing. Remote push notifications and Apple in-app purchases are not enabled in this version unless a later release states otherwise.

Tracking and sharing

GearDrop does not implement third-party advertising tracking in this version. If you tap a Buy link, the product page opens in your browser and the merchant may process that visit under its own privacy policy.

Retention and control

Device-local watchlist data remains on your device until you remove it or uninstall the app. Price alerts remain stored until they are unsubscribed, expire, or are removed during service maintenance.

Contact

For privacy or data requests, use the support channel published with 001.100app.dev.
